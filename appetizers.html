Deviled Eggs

Ingredients

  • 6-12 large eggs
  • Mayonnaise : 1/4 to 1/2 cup
  • Mustard : yellow or Dijon, 1-2 tsp
  • Salt & Pepper
  • Paprika for garnish

Preparation Steps:

  • Boil eggs for 10-20 min,then cool in an ice bath before peeling and halving lengthwise.
  • Scoop out the yolks and mash them with wuth mayonnaise, mustared ,salt,pepper until smooth.
  • Spoon or pipe this creamy mixture back into the egg white hollows and finish with a light sprinkle of paprika.

Spinach Artichoke Dip

Ingredients

  • 10 oz frozen chopped spinach
  • 14 oz can artichoke hearts
  • 8 oz cream cheese
  • 1/2 cup sour cream or Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella and 1/2 cup grated Parmesan
  • Garlic : 1 tsp minced or powder

Preparation Steps:

  • Combine softened cream cheese,sour cream and mayonnaise with chooped spinach and drained artichoke hearts.
  • Stir in shredded mozzarella and Parmesan, then season with garlic powder and red pepper flakes.
  • Bake at 350°F(175°C) for about 25 min until the cheese is bubbly and lightly browned on top.

Chicken Wings

Ingredients

  • 2-3 Ibs chicken wings
  • 1/2 cup Buffalo hot sauce
  • unsalted butter : 1/4 to 1/2 cup
  • Garlic powder : 1/2 to 1 tsp
  • Salt & Pepper

Preparation Steps:

  • Pat chicken wings dry and bake them on a wise rack at 425°F(220°C) for about 45-60 min , turning halfway through for even crisping.
  • While they bake, whisk together melted butter and hot sauce in a large bowl .
  • Once the wings are cooked and crispy , toss them in the sauce until fully coated.

Jalapeno Poppers

Ingredients

  • 10-12 fresh jalapenos (halved and seeded).
  • 8 oz cream cheese.
  • 1 cup shredded cheese.
  • Optional: 10-12 slices of bacon (to wrap) or Panko breadcrumbs.

Preparation Steps:

  • Slice Fresh Jalapenos in half lengthwise and remove the seeds and membranes to control the heat.
  • Fill each half with a mixture of cream cheese and shredded cheddar, then top with breadcrumbsor wrap in a half-slice of bacon.
  • Bake at 400°F(200°C) for 15-20 min until the peppers are tender and the topping is golden.

Stuffed Mushrooms

Ingredients

  • 1 lb large white or cremini mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 tbsp butter or olive oil

Preparation Steps:

  • Remove the stems from large white or cremini mushrooms and finely chop the stems.
  • Sauté the chopped stems with garlic and herbs, then mix with breadcrumbs and Parmesan cheese.
  • Fill the mushroom caps with the mixture and bake at 375°F(190°C)for 20 minutes until the caps are tender and the stuffing is browned.

Crostini

Ingredients

  • 1 baguette (sliced and toasted)
  • 4–6 Roma tomatoes (diced)
  • Fresh basil leaves (chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • Extra virgin olive oil and balsamic glaze

Preparation Steps:

  • Dice fresh tomatoes and toss them with minced garlic, chopped basil, balsamic vinegar, and olive oil, then let the mixture sit for at least 15 minutes to meld flavors.
  • Slice a baguette into rounds, brush them with olive oil, and toast them in the oven until golden.
  • Spoon the tomato mixture onto the warm bread just before serving to keep it crisp.

Sliders

Ingredients

  • 1 pack (12 count) dinner rolls
  • 1/2 lb deli meat (ham or turkey)
  • 1/2 lb sliced cheese (Swiss or provolone)
  • Melted butter with garlic powder and poppy seeds (for the glaze)

Preparation Steps:

  • Slice a pack of dinner rolls in half horizontally without separating the individual buns and place the bottom half in a baking dish.
  • Layer on deli meats like ham and slices of cheese, then replace the top half of the rolls.
  • Brush the tops with melted butter seasoned with garlic or poppy seeds and bake at 350°F(175°C) for 15–20 minutes until the cheese is melted.

Shrimp Cocktail

Ingredients

  • 1 lb large shrimp (peeled and deveined, tails left on)
  • 1 cup ketchup (the base for the sauce)
  • 1–3 tbsp prepared horseradish (adjust for desired heat)
  • M1 tbsp lemon juice (freshly squeezed)
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • For the poaching liquid: Water, lemon slices, bay leaves, salt, and black peppercorns
  • Optional:A dash of hot sauce or chili sauce for extra zing.

Preparation Steps:

  • Bring a pot of water to a boil with the lemon slices, bay leaves, salt, and peppercorns.
  • Add the shrimp and poach them gently for about 3–5 minutes until they turn pink and opaque.
  • BImmediately transfer the cooked shrimp to an ice bath to stop the cooking and ensure they stay firm and snappy
  • For the cocktail sauce, whisk together the ketchup, horseradish, lemon juice, Worcestershire sauce, and any optional spices in a small bowl.
  • Chill both the shrimp and the sauce in the refrigerator for at least an hour before serving. Arrange the cold shrimp around the edge of a bowl or glass filled with the sauce and garnish with fresh lemon wedges.
